Apple's dominance in China is over, UBS says (AAPL)

Apple is hitting a wall in China, UBS analyst Steven Milunovich wrote in note to clients. The iPhone maker's presence in China peaked in 2015, and it has been downhill from there. "In 2015 the move to 4G and the new iPhone 6/6 Plus form factors drove significant growth for Apple at the expense of Samsung," Milunovich wrote. "We now expect flat China units and little long-term growth," he said.
